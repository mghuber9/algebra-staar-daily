Day 12 – Slope From a Graph

Goal: Practice skills with Slope From a Graph.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Slope from a Graph):
From a graph, slope is rise over run: m = (change in y) ÷ (change in x). Pick two clear points on the line.

Example (General Algebra)

A line passes through the points (1, 2) and (4, 8) on a graph. Find its slope.

Step 1 – Label the points: (x₁, y₁) = (1, 2), (x₂, y₂) = (4, 8).

Step 2 – Compute rise and run:

  • rise = y₂ − y₁ = 8 − 2 = 6
  • run = x₂ − x₁ = 4 − 1 = 3

Step 3 – Slope: m = rise ÷ run = 6 ÷ 3 = 2.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A line passes through (−1, 4) and (3, −4). Find the slope.

Show answer

rise = −4 − 4 = −8
run = 3 − (−1) = 4
m = −8 ÷ 4 = −2.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A line on a coordinate grid goes through the points (0, 1) and (2, 5). What is the slope of the line?

  1. 2
  2. 3
  3. 4
  4. 5

2. A line on a graph decreases from left to right. It passes through (−3, 6) and (0, 0). What is its slope?

  1. −2
  2. −1
  3. 0
  4. 2

3. A line passes through (4, 3) and (8, 3). Which statement about the slope of the line is true?

  1. The slope is positive.
  2. The slope is negative.
  3. The slope is zero.
  4. The slope is undefined.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. (0, 1) and (2, 5): rise = 5 − 1 = 4, run = 2 − 0 = 2 → m = 4 ÷ 2 = 2 → A.

2. (−3, 6) and (0, 0): rise = 0 − 6 = −6, run = 0 − (−3) = 3 → m = −6 ÷ 3 = −2 → A.

3. (4, 3) and (8, 3): rise = 3 − 3 = 0, run = 8 − 4 = 4 → m = 0 ÷ 4 = 0 → C, horizontal line.

On any test, if the y-values are the same, slope is 0. If the x-values are the same, slope is undefined.
