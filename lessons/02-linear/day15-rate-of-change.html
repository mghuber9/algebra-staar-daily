Day 15 – Rate of Change

Goal: Practice skills with Rate of Change.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Rate of Change):
Rate of change is another word for slope in a real-world situation: rate of change = change in output ÷ change in input.

Example (General Algebra)

A water tank is being filled. After 2 minutes, it contains 50 liters. After 5 minutes, it contains 110 liters. What is the rate of change in liters per minute?

Step 1 – Treat as two points: (2, 50) and (5, 110), where x = time, y = liters.

Step 2 – Find changes:

  • Δy = 110 − 50 = 60
  • Δx = 5 − 2 = 3

Step 3 – Rate of change: 60 ÷ 3 = 20 liters per minute.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A plant is 12 cm tall after 1 week and 24 cm tall after 4 weeks. What is its average growth rate in cm per week?

Show answer

Δy = 24 − 12 = 12, Δx = 4 − 1 = 3 → 12 ÷ 3 = 4 cm per week.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A car travels 120 miles in 3 hours. What is the rate of change in miles per hour?

  1. 30
  2. 40
  3. 60
  4. 90

2. The table shows the number of pages a student has read over time.

Time (hours) Pages read
1 18
3 54

What does the rate of change represent in this situation?

  1. The total number of pages in the book
  2. The number of hours the student reads
  3. The number of pages the student reads per hour
  4. The number of pages left to read

3. The balance in a savings account is shown.

Month Balance ($)
1 200
4 260

What is the rate of change of the account balance per month?

  1. $10 per month
  2. $15 per month
  3. $20 per month
  4. $30 per month
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 120 ÷ 3 = 40 → B (40 miles per hour).

2. Rate of change is pages ÷ hours: it represents pages per hour → C.

3. Δy = 260 − 200 = 60, Δx = 4 − 1 = 3 → 60 ÷ 3 = 20 → C.

Connect the language “per” to rate of change: dollars per hour, miles per gallon, etc.
