Day 22 – Meaning of m and b in Word Problems

Goal: Practice skills with Meaning of m and b in Word Problems.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Meaning of m and b):
In a linear model y = mx + b:
  • m is the rate of change (change in y for each 1 unit of x).
  • b is the starting value or initial amount when x = 0.

Example (General Algebra)

A gym charges a $25 membership fee plus $10 per workout session. The total cost C (in dollars) for x sessions is modeled by C = 10x + 25.

What do 10 and 25 represent?

  • m = 10 → the cost per workout session.
  • b = 25 → the membership fee before any sessions.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A taxi ride costs $3 to start plus $2 per mile. The total cost is modeled by C = 2x + 3, where x is miles.

Describe the meaning of 2 and 3 in this situation.

Show answer

2 is the cost per mile.
3 is the starting fee (cost when you enter the taxi and go 0 miles).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. The equation y = 15x + 40 models the total cost y (in dollars) to rent a bike for x hours. What does the 40 represent?

  1. The cost per hour
  2. The number of hours
  3. The total cost
  4. The starting fee

2. The equation P = 50t + 200 models the profit P a business makes after t weeks. What does the 50 represent?

  1. The starting profit
  2. The profit each week
  3. The total profit after one year
  4. The number of items sold

3. A cell phone company charges according to the equation C = 0.05m + 30, where C is the total monthly cost (in dollars) and m is the number of text messages sent. Which statement is true?

  1. $30 is the cost per text message.
  2. 0.05 is the monthly fee.
  3. 0.05 is the cost per text message.
  4. $30 is the total cost every month.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. y = 15x + 40 → b = 40 is the cost when x = 0 → the starting fee → D.

2. 50 is the amount P increases each week → profit each week → B.

3. 0.05 is multiplied by m (texts), so it is the cost per text message → C.

On STAAR, always ask: “What is x? What is y? So what would one unit of x and x = 0 mean in this story?”
