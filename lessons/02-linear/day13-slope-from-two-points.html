Day 13 – Slope From Two Points

Goal: Practice skills with Slope From Two Points.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Slope from Two Points):
Use the slope formula: m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁). Be consistent with which point is “1” and “2”.

Example (General Algebra)

Find the slope of the line that passes through (−2, 1) and (4, 7).

Step 1 – Label points: (x₁, y₁) = (−2, 1), (x₂, y₂) = (4, 7).

Step 2 – Plug into formula:

m = (7 − 1) / (4 − (−2)) = 6 / 6 = 1.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Find the slope of the line through (3, −5) and (−1, 3).

Show answer

m = (3 − (−5)) / (−1 − 3) = 8 / (−4) = −2.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the slope of the line that passes through (1, 2) and (5, 10)?

  1. 2
  2. 4
  3. 6
  4. 8

2. Which ordered pair, when used with (−2, −1), gives a slope of 3?

  1. (0, 5)
  2. (0, −7)
  3. (1, 2)
  4. (1, −4)

3. A line has a slope of −1 and passes through (4, 2). Which point also lies on this line?

  1. (5, 3)
  2. (5, 1)
  3. (3, 1)
  4. (3, 3)
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. m = (10 − 2) / (5 − 1) = 8/4 = 2 → A.

2. Use m = 3. Try (0, 5): m = (5 − (−1)) / (0 − (−2)) = 6/2 = 3 → A works.

3. Slope −1 means “down 1, right 1”. From (4, 2) to (5, 1) is down 1, right 1 → B.

Encourage students to write the formula every time at first to avoid sign mistakes.
