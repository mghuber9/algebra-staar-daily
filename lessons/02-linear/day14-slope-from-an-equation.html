Day 14 – Slope From an Equation

Goal: Practice skills with Slope From an Equation.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Slope from an Equation):
For a line written as y = mx + b, the slope is the coefficient of x, m. If the equation is not in this form, rewrite it.

Example (General Algebra)

Find the slope of the line given by 2y − 6x = 8.

Step 1 – Solve for y:

2y − 6x = 8

2y = 6x + 8

y = 3x + 4

Step 2 – Identify slope: m = 3.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Find the slope of the line: 4x + 2y = 10.

Show answer

2y = −4x + 10 → y = −2x + 5 → slope m = −2.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the slope of the line represented by y = −5x + 3?

  1. −5
  2. 5
  3. −3
  4. 3

2. Which equation represents a line with slope 1/2?

  1. y = 2x − 4
  2. y = 4x + 1
  3. y = (1/2)x + 3
  4. y = −(1/2)x + 3

3. A line has equation 3x + y = 9. What is the slope of this line?

  1. −3
  2. 3
  3. −1/3
  4. 1/3
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. y = −5x + 3 → m = −5 → A.

2. Slope is the coefficient of x. Only C has slope 1/2.

3. 3x + y = 9 → y = −3x + 9 → slope is −3 → A.

If students are unsure, have them circle the coefficient of x after writing y = mx + b.
