Day 26 – Perpendicular Lines

Goal: Practice skills with Perpendicular Lines.

Key Idea (Perpendicular Lines):
Two non-vertical lines are perpendicular if their slopes are opposite reciprocals.
Example: slope 2 and slope −1/2.

Example (General Algebra)

A line has equation y = (1/3)x − 4. What is the slope of any line perpendicular to this line?

Original slope m = 1/3.
Opposite reciprocal → flip the fraction and change the sign → slope = −3.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A line has slope −4. What is the slope of a line perpendicular to it?

Show answer

Reciprocal of 4 is 1/4, change sign: slope = 1/4.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which line is perpendicular to y = 2x + 1?

  1. y = 2x − 4
  2. y = −2x + 3
  3. y = −(1/2)x + 1
  4. y = (1/2)x − 3

2. A line passes through (0, 5) and (2, 9). Which equation represents a line perpendicular to this line?

  1. y = 2x + 1
  2. y = −2x + 1
  3. y = (1/2)x + 1
  4. y = −(1/2)x + 1

3. The slope of a line is 3/4. What must be the slope of a line perpendicular to it?

  1. 4/3
  2. −3/4
  3. −4/3
  4. 3/4
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Perpendicular to slope 2 → slope must be −1/2 → C.

2. Slope of original line: (9 − 5) ÷ (2 − 0) = 4/2 = 2. Perpendicular slope = −1/2 → equation with that slope and some intercept: D is correct form.

3. Opposite reciprocal of 3/4 is −4/3 → C.

Quick memory trick: parallel → “same m”, perpendicular → “flip & change sign”.
