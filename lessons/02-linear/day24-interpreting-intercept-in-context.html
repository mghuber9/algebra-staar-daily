Key Idea (Interpreting Intercept):
The y-intercept in a linear model is the value of y when x = 0. It often represents a starting amount, initial fee, or initial condition.

Example (General Algebra)

A cell phone bill is modeled by B = 0.10m + 25, where B is the bill in dollars and m is the number of minutes used.

What does the y-intercept 25 represent?

When m = 0, B = 25. That means the customer pays $25 even if no minutes are used. This is the basic monthly fee.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A car rental cost is given by C = 40d + 60, where C is cost in dollars and d is the number of days. What does the 60 represent?

Show answer

When d = 0, C = 60. The $60 is the initial fee (cost to rent even before any days are counted).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. The function y = 5x + 20 models the total cost (in dollars) of a field trip, where x is the number of students. What does the 20 represent?

  1. The cost per student
  2. The number of students
  3. The cost for the teacher and bus before students
  4. The total cost for 4 students

2. A function that represents the height h (in feet) of a balloon is h = 4t + 10, where t is time in seconds after it is released. What does the y-intercept 10 represent?

  1. The balloon rises 10 feet every second.
  2. The balloon starts at a height of 10 feet.
  3. The balloon will be 10 feet high after 4 seconds.
  4. The balloon starts on the ground.

3. A function that models the number of people at a concert is P = 50t + 200, where t is the number of hours since the doors opened. What does the 200 represent?

  1. The number of people who enter each hour
  2. The total number of people who will attend
  3. The number of people who leave each hour
  4. The number of people already inside when doors open
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. When x = 0, y = 20. The 20 is a fixed cost (teacher/bus) before students → C.

2. When t = 0, h = 10. So the balloon starts at 10 feet high → B.

3. When t = 0, P = 200. So 200 people are already inside at the start → D.

Intercept questions are often “What happens at the beginning of the situation?” or “What happens when x = 0?”
