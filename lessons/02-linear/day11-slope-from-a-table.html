Day 11 – Slope From a Table

Goal: Practice skills with Slope From a Table.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Slope from a Table):
To find slope from a table, use m = Δy ÷ Δx: pick two points, find the change in y and the change in x, then do m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁).

Example (General Algebra)

The table shows the relationship between x and y.

x y
1 4
3 10

Find the slope of the line that passes through these points.

Step 1 – Pick two points: (1, 4) and (3, 10).

Step 2 – Find Δy and Δx:

  • Δy = 10 − 4 = 6
  • Δx = 3 − 1 = 2

Step 3 – Compute slope: m = Δy ÷ Δx = 6 ÷ 2 = 3.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Use the table to find the slope.

x y
−2 5
1 −1
Show answer

Points: (−2, 5) and (1, −1).
Δy = −1 − 5 = −6
Δx = 1 − (−2) = 3
m = −6 ÷ 3 = −2.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. The table shows the cost y (in dollars) to rent a bike for x hours.

x (hours) y (dollars)
1 12
3 28

What is the rate of change (slope) of the cost with respect to time?

  1. 4 dollars per hour
  2. 5 dollars per hour
  3. 8 dollars per hour
  4. 16 dollars per hour

2. A function is represented in the table.

x f(x)
0 −3
2 1
4 5

What is the slope of the line that models this function?

  1. −2
  2. −1
  3. 1
  4. 2

3. The table shows the distance y (in miles) a car travels after x hours.

x (hours) y (miles)
1 50
2 100
3 150

Which statement best describes the slope of the line that represents this relationship?

  1. The car travels 25 miles each hour.
  2. The car travels 50 miles each hour.
  3. The car travels 100 miles each hour.
  4. The car travels 150 miles each hour.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Use points (1, 12) and (3, 28): Δy = 28 − 12 = 16, Δx = 3 − 1 = 2 → m = 16 ÷ 2 = 8 → C.

2. Use (0, −3) and (2, 1): Δy = 1 − (−3) = 4, Δx = 2 − 0 = 2 → m = 4 ÷ 2 = 2 → D.

3. (1, 50) and (2, 100): Δy = 100 − 50 = 50, Δx = 2 − 1 = 1 → m = 50 → B.

Tip: When possible, choose table rows that make the subtraction easy (like x-values that are close together).
