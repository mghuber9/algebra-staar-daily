Day 27 – Direct Variation (y = kx)

Goal: Practice skills with Direct Variation (y = kx).

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Direct Variation):
A direct variation has the form y = kx where k is the constant of variation. The graph always passes through the origin (0, 0).

Example (General Algebra)

Suppose y varies directly with x, and y = 18 when x = 6. Find the equation.

Step 1 – Use y = kx: 18 = k(6) → k = 3.

Step 2 – Equation: y = 3x.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

y varies directly with x. If y = 40 when x = 5, find the equation that represents the variation.

Show answer

40 = k(5) → k = 8 → equation: y = 8x.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which equation represents a direct variation?

  1. y = 4x + 2
  2. y = 7
  3. y = −5x
  4. y = x − 3

2. In a direct variation, y = kx. If y = 12 when x = 3, what is the value of k?

  1. 3
  2. 4
  3. 9
  4. 36

3. A worker earns wages that vary directly with the number of hours worked. She earns $72 for 6 hours. Which equation represents her hourly pay?

  1. y = 6x
  2. y = 8x
  3. y = 12x
  4. y = 72x
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Direct variation must be y = kx; no constant term. Only C fits (y = −5x).

2. 12 = k(3) → k = 4 → B.

3. 72 = k(6) → k = 12 → wages y = 12x → C.

If the graph does not pass through (0, 0), it is not a direct variation.
