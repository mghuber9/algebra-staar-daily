Day 16 – Types of Slope

Goal: Practice skills with Types of Slope.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Types of Slope):
• Positive slope: line rises from left to right.
• Negative slope: line falls from left to right.
• Zero slope: horizontal line.
• Undefined slope: vertical line.

Example (General Algebra)

Classify the slope of each graph description:

  • A line goes up as you move to the right.
  • A line goes down as you move to the right.
  • A line is perfectly horizontal.
  • A line is perfectly vertical.

Answers:

  • Up to the right → positive slope.
  • Down to the right → negative slope.
  • Horizontal → zero slope.
  • Vertical → undefined slope.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A line passes through (−2, 4) and (2, 4). What type of slope does it have?

Show answer

Both y-values are 4 → horizontal line → zero slope.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A line has a slope of −3. How does this line look on a graph?

  1. Horizontal, increasing from left to right
  2. Horizontal, constant from left to right
  3. Diagonal, rising from left to right
  4. Diagonal, falling from left to right

2. Which equation represents a line with an undefined slope?

  1. y = 5
  2. x = −2
  3. y = −3x + 1
  4. y = 0

3. The graph of a line passes through (0, 2) and (4, 2). Which statement is true?

  1. The line has positive slope and is increasing.
  2. The line has negative slope and is decreasing.
  3. The line has zero slope and is horizontal.
  4. The line has undefined slope and is vertical.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Negative slope → line falls from left to right → D.

2. x = constant is a vertical line → B has undefined slope.

3. y is always 2, so the line is horizontal → zero slope → C.

A quick memory trick: “HOY VUX” — Horizontal: 0 slope, y = constant; Vertical: Undefined slope, x = constant.
