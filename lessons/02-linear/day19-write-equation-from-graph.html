Day 19 – Write Equation From Graph

Goal: Practice skills with Write Equation From Graph.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Equation from a Graph):
To write y = mx + b from a line on a graph:
  1. Find the y-intercept (where the line crosses the y-axis).
  2. Pick another point and compute the slope using rise/run.
  3. Write y = mx + b using your slope and intercept.

Example (General Algebra)

A line crosses the y-axis at (0, −2) and goes through (3, 4). Find its equation.

Step 1 – y-intercept: b = −2.

Step 2 – Slope:

  • rise = 4 − (−2) = 6
  • run = 3 − 0 = 3
  • m = 6 ÷ 3 = 2

Step 3 – Equation: y = 2x − 2.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A line crosses the y-axis at (0, 5) and passes through (2, 1). Write its equation in slope-intercept form.

Show answer

b = 5.
m = (1 − 5) / (2 − 0) = −4/2 = −2.
Equation: y = −2x + 5.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A line on a graph crosses the y-axis at (0, 3) and passes through (4, 11). Which equation represents this line?

  1. y = 2x + 3
  2. y = 3x + 2
  3. y = 4x + 3
  4. y = 2x − 5

2. A line has y-intercept −4 and passes through (2, 2). What is its equation?

  1. y = 3x − 4
  2. y = 2x − 4
  3. y = 4x − 2
  4. y = 3x + 4

3. The graph of a line shows points at (0, −1) and (−2, 3). Which equation matches this line?

  1. y = 2x − 1
  2. y = −2x − 1
  3. y = −2x + 3
  4. y = 2x + 3
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. m = (11 − 3) / (4 − 0) = 8/4 = 2, b = 3 → y = 2x + 3 → A.

2. b = −4, m = (2 − (−4)) / (2 − 0) = 6/2 = 3 → y = 3x − 4 → A.

3. b = −1 (from (0, −1)), m = (3 − (−1)) / (−2 − 0) = 4 / (−2) = −2 → y = −2x − 1 → B.

Encourage students: “Read b directly from the y-axis, then compute m carefully using two points.”
