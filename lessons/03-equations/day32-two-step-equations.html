Day 32 – Two-Step Equations

Goal: Practice skills with Two-Step Equations.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Two-Step Equations):
For two-step equations, undo addition or subtraction first, then undo multiplication or division to isolate the variable.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the equation: 3x + 5 = 20

Step 1 – Undo addition: Subtract 5 from both sides.

  • 3x + 5 − 5 = 20 − 5
  • 3x = 15

Step 2 – Undo multiplication: Divide both sides by 3.

  • 3x ÷ 3 = 15 ÷ 3
  • x = 5

Check: 3(5) + 5 = 15 + 5 = 20 ✔

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve: 4x − 7 = 21

Show answer

Add 7 to both sides: 4x − 7 + 7 = 21 + 7 → 4x = 28.
Divide by 4: x = 28 ÷ 4 = 7.
Answer: x = 7.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve for x: 5x + 2 = 27

  1. x = 5
  2. x = 6
  3. x = 7
  4. x = 25

2. A gym charges a $15 sign-up fee plus $8 per month. The total cost after m months is given by 8m + 15 = 71. How many months of membership is this?

  1. 5 months
  2. 6 months
  3. 7 months
  4. 8 months

3. Solve: −2x + 9 = 1

  1. x = 4
  2. x = −4
  3. x = −5
  4. x = 5
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 5x + 2 = 27 → subtract 2: 5x = 25 → divide by 5: x = 5 → A.

2. 8m + 15 = 71 → subtract 15: 8m = 56 → divide by 8: m = 7 → C.

3. −2x + 9 = 1 → subtract 9: −2x = 1 − 9 = −8 → divide by −2: x = 4 → A.

A common mistake is dividing before removing the constant term—always clear the +/− number first.
