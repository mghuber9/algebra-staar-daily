Day 33 – Multi-Step Equations

Goal: Practice skills with Multi-Step Equations.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Multi-Step Equations):
For multi-step equations, simplify both sides first (distribute, combine like terms), then solve using the same order as two-step equations.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the equation: 2(3x − 1) + 4 = 20

Step 1 – Distribute:

  • 2(3x − 1) + 4 = 20
  • 6x − 2 + 4 = 20
  • 6x + 2 = 20

Step 2 – Undo addition: Subtract 2 from both sides.

  • 6x + 2 − 2 = 20 − 2
  • 6x = 18

Step 3 – Undo multiplication: Divide both sides by 6.

  • x = 18 ÷ 6 = 3

Check: 2(3(3) − 1) + 4 = 2(9 − 1) + 4 = 2(8) + 4 = 16 + 4 = 20 ✔

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve: 3(x + 2) − 5 = 16

Show answer

Distribute: 3x + 6 − 5 = 16 → 3x + 1 = 16.
Subtract 1: 3x = 15.
Divide by 3: x = 5.
Answer: x = 5.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve for x: 4(x − 3) + 2 = 18

  1. x = 4
  2. x = 5
  3. x = 6
  4. x = 7

2. A taxi company charges $3 to start a ride plus $2 for each mile. The total cost is represented by 2m + 3 = 19, where m is the number of miles. How many miles was the ride?

  1. 6 miles
  2. 7 miles
  3. 8 miles
  4. 9 miles

3. Solve: 5x − 2(x + 1) = 7

  1. x = 1
  2. x = 2
  3. x = 3
  4. x = 4
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 4(x − 3) + 2 = 18 → 4x − 12 + 2 = 18 → 4x − 10 = 18 → add 10: 4x = 28 → x = 7 → D.

2. 2m + 3 = 19 → subtract 3: 2m = 16 → divide by 2: m = 8 → C.

3. 5x − 2(x + 1) = 7 → 5x − 2x − 2 = 7 → 3x − 2 = 7 → add 2: 3x = 9 → x = 3 → C.

Always simplify each side first—distribute and combine like terms—before trying to “solve” the equation.
