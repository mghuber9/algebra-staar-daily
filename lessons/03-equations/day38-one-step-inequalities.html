Day 38 – One-Step Inequalities

Goal: Practice skills with One-Step Inequalities.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (One-Step Inequalities):
Solve one-step inequalities just like one-step equations using inverse operations. The inequality symbol stays the same—unless you multiply or divide by a negative.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the inequality: x + 4 > 9

Step 1 – Undo addition: Subtract 4 from both sides.

  • x + 4 − 4 > 9 − 4
  • x > 5

Meaning: All numbers greater than 5 make the inequality true.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve: x − 7 ≤ 2

Show answer

Add 7 to both sides: x − 7 + 7 ≤ 2 + 7 → x ≤ 9.
Answer: x ≤ 9.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve for x: x / 3 ≥ 4

  1. x ≥ 1
  2. x ≥ 7
  3. x ≥ 12
  4. x ≥ 16

2. A movie theater requires customers to be at least 13 years old to see a PG-13 movie alone. Which inequality represents this situation, where a is the customer’s age?

  1. a > 13
  2. a ≥ 13
  3. a < 13
  4. a ≤ 13

3. Solve the inequality: −5 + x < 2

  1. x < 7
  2. x > 7
  3. x ≤ 7
  4. x ≥ 7
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. x / 3 ≥ 4 → multiply both sides by 3: x ≥ 12 → C.

2. “At least 13” means 13 or older → a ≥ 13 → B.

3. −5 + x < 2 → add 5 to both sides: x < 7 → A.

For adding or subtracting, inequalities behave like equations—the symbol doesn’t flip unless you multiply or divide by a negative.
