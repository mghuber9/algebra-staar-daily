Day 40 – Multi-Step Inequalities

Goal: Practice skills with Multi-Step Inequalities.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Multi-Step Inequalities):
For multi-step inequalities, simplify each side first (distribute, combine like terms), then solve. Remember: multiply or divide by a negative → flip the inequality sign.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the inequality: 2(x + 3) − 5 > 7

Step 1 – Distribute:

  • 2(x + 3) − 5 > 7
  • 2x + 6 − 5 > 7
  • 2x + 1 > 7

Step 2 – Undo addition: Subtract 1 from both sides.

  • 2x > 6

Step 3 – Undo multiplication: Divide both sides by 2.

  • x > 3

Meaning: All numbers greater than 3 make the inequality true.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve: 3(x − 2) + 4 ≤ 16

Show answer

Distribute: 3x − 6 + 4 ≤ 16 → 3x − 2 ≤ 16.
Add 2: 3x ≤ 18.
Divide by 3: x ≤ 6.
Answer: x ≤ 6.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve: 4(x − 1) + 2 < 18

  1. x < 3
  2. x < 4
  3. x < 5
  4. x < 6

2. A school is renting buses for a field trip. Each bus holds 50 students, and there is a $120 fee per bus plus $5 per student. The inequality 5s + 120 ≤ 320 represents the total cost, where s is the number of students. What is the greatest number of students that can go on the trip while keeping the cost at or below $320?

  1. 30 students
  2. 32 students
  3. 40 students
  4. 50 students

3. Solve: −3(2x − 1) ≥ 9

  1. x ≥ −1
  2. x ≤ −1
  3. x ≥ 2
  4. x ≤ 2
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 4(x − 1) + 2 < 18 → 4x − 4 + 2 < 18 → 4x − 2 < 18 → add 2: 4x < 20 → divide by 4: x < 5 → C.

2. 5s + 120 ≤ 320 → subtract 120: 5s ≤ 200 → divide by 5: s ≤ 40, so the greatest whole number is 40 → C.

3. −3(2x − 1) ≥ 9 → distribute: −6x + 3 ≥ 9 → subtract 3: −6x ≥ 6 → divide by −6 and flip the sign: x ≤ −1 → B.

Always do the algebra steps in the same order as equations—but keep an eye out for negative coefficients, since those cause the inequality symbol to flip.
