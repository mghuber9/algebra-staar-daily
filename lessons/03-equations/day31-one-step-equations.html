Goal: Practice skills with One-Step Equations.

Key Idea (One-Step Equations):
To solve a one-step equation, do the inverse (opposite) operation to isolate the variable. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the equation: x + 7 = 15

Step 1 – Identify the operation: 7 is being added to x.

Step 2 – Do the inverse operation: Subtract 7 from both sides.

  • x + 7 − 7 = 15 − 7
  • x = 8

Check: 8 + 7 = 15 ✔

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve: x − 9 = 4

Show answer

Add 9 to both sides: x − 9 + 9 = 4 + 9 → x = 13.
Answer: x = 13.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve for x: x ÷ 5 = 3

  1. x = 8
  2. x = 10
  3. x = 15
  4. x = 20

2. The equation 4x = 28 represents the cost of buying 4 identical notebooks. What is the cost of one notebook?

  1. $6
  2. $7
  3. $8
  4. $32

3. Solve: x + 12 = −3

  1. x = −15
  2. x = 9
  3. x = −9
  4. x = 15
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. x ÷ 5 = 3 → multiply both sides by 5: x = 3(5) = 15 → C.

2. 4x = 28 → divide both sides by 4: x = 28 ÷ 4 = 7, so each notebook costs $7 → B.

3. x + 12 = −3 → subtract 12 from both sides: x = −3 − 12 = −15 → A.

One-step equations always use exactly one inverse operation—just make sure you use the correct opposite.
