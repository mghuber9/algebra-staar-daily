Key Idea (Trend Lines):
A trend line shows the general direction of data over time. • Upward → increasing trend • Downward → decreasing trend • Flat → no change

Example (General Algebra)

A teacher tracks the number of missing assignments each week:

Week: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 Missing: 12, 10, 9, 7, 5

Trend: decreasing → the line would slope downward.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A scatterplot of monthly sales slopes upward. What does this show?

Show answer

Sales are generally increasing over time.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A trend line with positive slope indicates:

  1. Decrease in y over time
  2. Increase in y over time
  3. No pattern
  4. Constant y-values

2. A company’s profit trend line slopes downward. What does this mean?

  1. Profit is increasing
  2. Profit is decreasing
  3. Profit is constant
  4. Profit has no pattern

3. A flat trend line means:

  1. Rapid increase
  2. Rapid decrease
  3. No significant change
  4. Data has outliers
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Positive slope → increasing trend → B

2. Downward slope → decreasing profit → B

3. Flat line → about the same over time → C

Trend lines help predict future values—even if individual data points vary.
