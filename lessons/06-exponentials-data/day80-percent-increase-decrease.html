Day 80 – Percent Increase/Decrease

Goal: Practice skills with Percent Increase/Decrease.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Percent Increase/Decrease):
To apply a percent change, multiply by the growth/decay factor:
• Increase: new = original(1 + r)
• Decrease: new = original(1 − r)
where r is the percent as a decimal.

Example (General Algebra)

A jacket costs $60 and is on sale for 25% off. What is the sale price?

Step 1 – Convert percent to decimal: 25% = 0.25.

Step 2 – Use decrease formula:

  • Factor = 1 − 0.25 = 0.75
  • New price = 60(0.75) = 45

The sale price is $45.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A phone bill of $80 increases by 10%. What is the new bill?

Show answer

10% = 0.10 → factor = 1.10.
New bill = 80(1.10) = $88.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. The price of a video game increases from $40 to $50. What is the percent increase?

  1. 10%
  2. 20%
  3. 25%
  4. 50%

2. A store advertises “30% off all shoes.” Which expression gives the sale price S for original price p?

  1. S = p − 0.3
  2. S = p(1.3)
  3. S = p(0.3)
  4. S = p(0.7)

3. A population of 1,200 decreases by 8%. Which expression gives the new population?

  1. 1200 − 0.08
  2. 1200(1.08)
  3. 1200(0.92)
  4. 1200(0.08)
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Change = 50 − 40 = 10. Percent change = 10/40 = 0.25 = 25% → C.

2. 30% off → factor = 1 − 0.30 = 0.70 → S = p(0.7) → D.

3. 8% decrease → factor = 1 − 0.08 = 0.92 → new = 1200(0.92) → C.

“Off” or “discount” → 1 − r. “Mark-up” or “increase” → 1 + r. Train students to build the factor before multiplying.
