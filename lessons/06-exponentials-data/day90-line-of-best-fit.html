Day 90 – Line of Best Fit

Goal: Practice skills with Line of Best Fit.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Line of Best Fit):
A line of best fit models the overall trend in a scatterplot. It can be used to make predictions (interpolation/extrapolation).

Example (General Algebra)

You eyeball a line of best fit through points on a price–years scatterplot and estimate the model as: y = −2x + 50.

This means price decreases by $2 per year.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

If the line of best fit is y = 4x + 10, what is the predicted value when x = 3?

Show answer

y = 4(3) + 10 = 22.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A scatterplot shows a downward trend. What will the slope of the best-fit line be?

  1. Positive
  2. Negative
  3. Zero
  4. Undefined

2. The line of best fit is y = 1.5x + 2. What does the 1.5 represent?

  1. Initial value
  2. Rate of change
  3. Maximum value
  4. Median

3. If x = 10, what does the model predict?

  1. 15
  2. 17
  3. 20
  4. 25
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Downward trend → negative slope → B

2. Slope = 1.5 → change in y per 1 x → B

3. y = 1.5(10) + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17 → B

A best-fit line smooths the overall pattern—even if points don’t lie exactly on the line.
