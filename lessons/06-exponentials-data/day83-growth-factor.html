Key Idea (Growth Factor):
In exponential functions, the growth factor is the number you multiply by each time. • If something grows by r%, the growth factor is 1 + r. • If something decays by r%, the factor is 1 − r.

Example (General Algebra)

A population increases by 6% each year. What is the growth factor?

Step 1 – Convert percent to decimal: 6% = 0.06

Step 2 – Add to 1: Growth factor = 1 + 0.06 = 1.06

Your Turn (General Algebra)

If a quantity decreases by 15% each year, what is the decay factor?

Show answer

15% = 0.15 → factor = 1 − 0.15 = 0.85.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A bank account grows by 4%. What is the growth factor?

  1. 0.04
  2. 1.04
  3. 4
  4. 0.96

2. A quantity decreases by 12%. What is the decay factor?

  1. 0.12
  2. 1.12
  3. 0.88
  4. 0.78

3. If a function multiplies by 1.5 each step, what is the percent increase?

  1. 25%
  2. 33%
  3. 40%
  4. 50%
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 1 + 0.04 = 1.04 → B

2. 1 − 0.12 = 0.88 → C

3. 1.5 = 1 + r → r = 0.5 = 50% → D

Students mix up “percent” and “factor”—remind them the factor always starts with 1.
