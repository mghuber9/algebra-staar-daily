Key Idea (Scatterplots):
Scatterplots show the relationship between two variables. • Positive correlation: points trend upward • Negative correlation: points trend downward • No correlation: no pattern

Example (General Algebra)

If studying hours increase and test scores also increase, the scatterplot shows positive correlation.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A scatterplot slopes downward from left to right. What type of correlation is this?

Show answer

Downward trend → negative correlation.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which scatterplot shows no correlation?

  1. Points going up
  2. Points going down
  3. Points in no pattern
  4. Points in a straight line

2. If as x increases, y decreases, what correlation is this?

  1. Positive
  2. Negative
  3. No correlation
  4. Strong positive

3. Scatterplots are useful for:

  1. Showing frequency of values
  2. Showing trends between two variables
  3. Calculating exact solutions
  4. Showing median values
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. No visible pattern → C

2. Decreases with x → B

3. Scatterplots show trends → B

Correlation shows direction, not cause-and-effect.
