Day 78 – Growth Formula

Goal: Practice skills with Growth Formula.

Key Idea (Exponential Growth):
Exponential growth can be modeled by y = a(1 + r)t, where:
• a = starting amount
• r = growth rate (decimal)
• t = number of time periods

Example (General Algebra)

A population of 500 animals increases by 4% each year. Write a function and find the population after 3 years.

Step 1 – Identify a and r:

  • a = 500
  • r = 4% = 0.04

Step 2 – Write the model:

  • y = 500(1 + 0.04)t = 500(1.04)t

Step 3 – Find y after 3 years (t = 3):

  • y = 500(1.04)³ ≈ 500(1.124864) ≈ 562

After 3 years, the population is about 562 animals.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

A savings account starts with $800 and grows by 5% each year. Write a function for the balance B(t).

Show answer

a = 800, r = 0.05.
B(t) = 800(1.05)t.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which function shows an initial amount of 200 and a growth rate of 8% per year?

  1. y = 200(0.08)t
  2. y = 200(1.8)t
  3. y = 200(1.08)t
  4. y = 200(0.92)t

2. A town’s population is modeled by P(t) = 10,000(1.03)t. What does 1.03 represent?

  1. The starting population
  2. The yearly growth rate in percent
  3. The amount added each year
  4. The growth factor (100% + 3%) each year

3. A cell culture starts with 1,500 cells and grows by 2% per hour. Which expression gives the number of cells after 5 hours?

  1. 1500(1.02)5
  2. 1500(0.02)5
  3. 1500(1.2)5
  4. 1500 + 0.02(5)
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Growth factor is 1 + 0.08 = 1.08 → y = 200(1.08)t → C.

2. 1.03 is the growth factor (100% + 3%) → D.

3. a = 1500, r = 0.02 → model is 1500(1.02)t; for t = 5 → A.

Growth factor = 1 + r. If the percent is 6%, convert to 0.06 and use 1.06 as your multiplier.
