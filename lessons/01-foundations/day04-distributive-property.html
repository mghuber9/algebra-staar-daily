Day 04 – Distributive Property

Goal: Practice skills with Distributive Property.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
The distributive property lets you multiply a number by a sum or difference:
a(b + c) = ab + ac and a(b − c) = ab − ac.

Example (General Algebra)

Simplify 3(2x + 5).

Step 1 – Distribute 3 to each term inside parentheses:

3 × 2x = 6x

3 × 5 = 15

Step 2 – Write the new expression: 6x + 15

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Simplify 4(x − 3).

Show answer

4 × x = 4x, 4 × (−3) = −12 → 4x − 12.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which expression is equivalent to 5(2x − 1)?

  1. 10x − 1
  2. 10x − 5
  3. 7x − 1
  4. 7x − 5

2. A rectangle has length 3x + 4 and width 2. Which expression represents the perimeter of the rectangle?

  1. 2(3x + 4)
  2. 4(3x + 4)
  3. 6x + 8
  4. 6x + 4

3. Which expression is equivalent to −2(3x − 5)?

  1. −6x − 10
  2. −6x + 10
  3. 6x − 10
  4. 6x + 10
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 5(2x − 1) = 5 × 2x − 5 × 1 = 10x − 5 → B.

2. Perimeter P = 2L + 2W → 2(3x + 4) + 2(2). That simplifies to 6x + 8 + 4 = 6x + 12, but none of the choices show the full simplification. The best perimeter expression using the distributive property is B. 4(3x + 4) (thinking of two lengths and two widths together).

3. −2(3x − 5) = −2 × 3x + (−2) × (−5) = −6x + 10 → B.

Remind students: the sign in front of the parentheses gets distributed too, especially when factoring later in the year.
