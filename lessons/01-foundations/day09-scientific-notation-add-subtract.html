Day 09 – Scientific Notation (Add/Subtract)

Goal: Practice skills with Scientific Notation (Add/Subtract).

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
To add or subtract numbers in scientific notation, the exponents on 10 must match. Rewrite one number so both have the same power of 10, then add or subtract the a-values.

Example (General Algebra)

Simplify (3.5 × 106) + (1.2 × 106).

Step 1 – Check exponents: both are 106, so we can add a-values.

Step 2 – Add the a-values: 3.5 + 1.2 = 4.7.

Answer: 4.7 × 106.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Simplify (6.8 × 105) − (2.3 × 105).

Show answer

6.8 − 2.3 = 4.5 → 4.5 × 105.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which expression shows the sum of 4.2 × 107 and 3.1 × 107 in scientific notation?

  1. 7.3 × 107
  2. 7.3 × 1014
  3. 1.3 × 108
  4. 7.3 × 106

2. A scientist measured two populations of bacteria. One sample had 5.0 × 108 bacteria, and another had 2.5 × 108 bacteria. What is the total number of bacteria in scientific notation?

  1. 7.5 × 108
  2. 7.5 × 1016
  3. 2.5 × 1016
  4. 2.0 × 108

3. The mass of Planet A is 6.0 × 1024 kilograms and Planet B is 1.4 × 1024 kilograms. What is the difference in their masses in scientific notation?

  1. 7.4 × 1024
  2. 4.6 × 1024
  3. 4.6 × 1025
  4. 7.4 × 1023
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. (4.2 + 3.1) × 107 = 7.3 × 107 → A.

2. (5.0 + 2.5) × 108 = 7.5 × 108 → A.

3. 6.0 − 1.4 = 4.6 → 4.6 × 1024 → B.

Stress that adding/subtracting scientific notation is like combining like terms – exponents must match.
