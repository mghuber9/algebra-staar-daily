Day 06 – Exponent Basics

Goal: Practice skills with Exponent Basics.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
An exponent tells you how many times to multiply a base by itself. For example, 34 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3.

Example (General Algebra)

Evaluate 23.

Step 1 – Read the exponent: 23 means 2 multiplied by itself 3 times.

2 × 2 × 2

Step 2 – Multiply: 2 × 2 = 4, then 4 × 2 = 8.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Evaluate 52.

Show answer

52 = 5 × 5 = 25.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which expression is equal to 34?

  1. 3 × 4
  2. 4 × 3
  3. 3 × 3 × 3 × 3
  4. 4 × 4 × 4

2. What is the value of 102?

  1. 12
  2. 20
  3. 100
  4. 102

3. A square has side length 7 units. Which expression represents the area of the square, and what is its value?

  1. 72; 14
  2. 72; 49
  3. 2 × 7; 14
  4. 2 × 7; 49
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 34 means 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 → C.

2. 102 = 10 × 10 = 100 → C.
102 is a common mistake from reading it as a two-digit number, not an exponent.

3. Area of a square is side2 → 72 = 49 → B.

Emphasize: exponents are repeated multiplication, not multiplication by the exponent.
