Day 08 – Scientific Notation (Multiply/Divide)

Goal: Practice skills with Scientific Notation (Multiply/Divide).

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
Scientific notation has the form a × 10n where 1 ≤ a < 10 and n is an integer.
• When multiplying: multiply the a-values and add the exponents.
• When dividing: divide the a-values and subtract the exponents.

Example (General Algebra – Multiply)

Simplify (3 × 104) × (2 × 103) in scientific notation.

Step 1 – Multiply the a-values: 3 × 2 = 6.

Step 2 – Add the exponents: 104 × 103 = 104+3 = 107.

So the product is 6 × 107.

Your Turn (General Algebra – Divide)

Simplify (8 × 106) ÷ (4 × 102).

Show answer

8 ÷ 4 = 2, and 106 ÷ 102 = 106−2 = 104.
Answer: 2 × 104.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which expression shows the product of (4 × 105) and (3 × 102) in scientific notation?

  1. 7 × 107
  2. 12 × 107
  3. 1.2 × 108
  4. 12 × 103

2. A scientist multiplies 6 × 103 by 5 × 104. What is the result in scientific notation?

  1. 11 × 107
  2. 3 × 108
  3. 30 × 107
  4. 3.0 × 108

3. Which expression shows the quotient of (9 × 107) and (3 × 103) in scientific notation?

  1. 3 × 104
  2. 27 × 1010
  3. 3 × 1010
  4. 3 × 105
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. (4 × 105)(3 × 102)
4 × 3 = 12, exponents: 105+2 = 107 → 12 × 107 = 1.2 × 108 → C.

2. (6 × 103)(5 × 104) = 30 × 107 = 3.0 × 108 → D.

3. (9 × 107) ÷ (3 × 103)
9 ÷ 3 = 3, exponents: 107−3 = 104 → 3 × 104 → A.

Reminder: After multiplying or dividing, make sure the leading number is between 1 and 10; adjust the exponent if needed.
