Day 07 – Zero & Negative Exponents

Goal: Practice skills with Zero & Negative Exponents.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
For any nonzero number a:
• a0 = 1
• a−n = 1 ÷ an

Example (General Algebra)

Evaluate 50 and 2−3.

Step 1 – Zero exponent: 50 = 1.

Step 2 – Negative exponent: 2−3 = 1 ÷ 23 = 1 ÷ 8 = 1/8.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Evaluate 100 and 3−2.

Show answer

100 = 1
3−2 = 1 ÷ 32 = 1 ÷ 9 = 1/9.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the value of 70?

  1. 0
  2. 1
  3. 7
  4. −7

2. Which expression is equivalent to 4−2?

  1. 4 × 4
  2. 1 ÷ 4
  3. 1 ÷ 42
  4. −42

3. A student wrote that 2−3 = −8. Which statement best explains the student’s error?

  1. The student added the exponent and the base.
  2. The student multiplied incorrectly.
  3. The student thought a negative exponent makes the value negative instead of a fraction.
  4. The student forgot that 23 = 6.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. a0 = 1 for any nonzero a → B.

2. 4−2 = 1 ÷ 42 = 1 ÷ 16 → C.
Choice B (1 ÷ 4) would be 4−1.

3. 2−3 = 1 ÷ 23, not −(23). The student treated the negative exponent as a negative answer, not as a reciprocal → C.

Highlight that negative exponents create fractions (reciprocals), not negative numbers by default.
