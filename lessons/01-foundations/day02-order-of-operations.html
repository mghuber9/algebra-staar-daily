Day 02 – Order of Operations

Goal: Practice skills with Order of Operations.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
Use the order of operations (PEMDAS): Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (from left to right), Addition and Subtraction (from left to right).

Example (General Algebra)

Simplify 6 + 2(5 − 3).

Step 1 – Parentheses: 5 − 3 = 2 → 6 + 2(2)

Step 2 – Multiplication: 2 × 2 = 4 → 6 + 4

Step 3 – Addition: 6 + 4 = 10

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Simplify 4(3 + 1) − 5.

Show answer

3 + 1 = 4 → 4(4) − 5 = 16 − 5 = 11.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the value of 3 + 4 × 2?

  1. 14
  2. 11
  3. 10
  4. 7

2. A student simplified the expression 5(2 + 6) − 4 and got an answer of 16. What is the correct value of the expression?

  1. 16
  2. 36
  3. 28
  4. 24

3. Which expression has the same value as 23 + 4 × 3?

  1. 8 + 12
  2. 16 + 3
  3. 2 × (3 + 4) × 3
  4. (2 + 3)2 + 4
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 3 + 4 × 2 → do multiplication first: 4 × 2 = 8 → 3 + 8 = 11 → B.
A (14) from doing 3 + 4 = 7, then 7 × 2 = 14.

2. 5(2 + 6) − 4 → 2 + 6 = 8 → 5(8) − 4 = 40 − 4 = 36 → B.
16 comes from 5(2) + 6 − 4 (wrong order).

3. 23 + 4 × 3 = 8 + 12 = 20. Only A (8 + 12) shows the same operations in the correct order.

Emphasize: multiplication and division come before addition and subtraction, but are read left to right.
