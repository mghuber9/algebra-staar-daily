Day 01 – Evaluating Expressions

Goal: Practice skills with Evaluating Expressions.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
To evaluate an expression, substitute the given values for each variable and then simplify using the correct order of operations.

Example (General Algebra)

Evaluate the expression 3x + 4 when x = 5.

Step 1 – Substitute: Replace x with 5.

3(5) + 4

Step 2 – Multiply: 3 × 5 = 15

15 + 4

Step 3 – Add: 15 + 4 = 19

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Evaluate 2x − 7 when x = −3.

Show answer

Substitute: 2(−3) − 7 = −6 − 7 = −13.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. The expression 4x + 2 represents the cost in dollars to enter a park, where x is the number of tickets purchased. What is the value of the expression when x = 3?

  1. $10
  2. $12
  3. $14
  4. $18

2. A taxi company charges a flat fee of $5 plus $2 per mile. The total cost in dollars is given by 2m + 5, where m is the number of miles. What is the total cost for a 6-mile trip?

  1. $11
  2. $12
  3. $15
  4. $17

3. The value of a coin collection in dollars is given by 8n − 3, where n is the number of special coins. What is the value of the collection when n = 4?

  1. $13
  2. $17
  3. $29
  4. $32
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 4x + 2, x = 3 → 4(3) + 2 = 12 + 2 = 14 → C.
A (10) comes from doing 4 + 2 × 3 incorrectly.
B (12) from 4 × 3 only.
D (18) from 4 × 3 + 6 (adding x instead of 2).

2. 2m + 5, m = 6 → 2(6) + 5 = 12 + 5 = 17 → D.

3. 8n − 3, n = 4 → 8(4) − 3 = 32 − 3 = 29 → C.
A (13) from 8 + 4 − 3.
B (17) from 8 + 4 × 3.
D (32) from 8 × 4 only.
