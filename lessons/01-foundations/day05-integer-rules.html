Day 05 – Integer Rules

Goal: Practice skills with Integer Rules.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea:
When working with integers:
• Adding same signs: add and keep the sign.
• Adding different signs: subtract and keep the sign of the larger absolute value.
• Multiplying or dividing: same signs → positive, different signs → negative.

Example (General Algebra)

Simplify −7 + 12.

Different signs → subtract absolute values: 12 − 7 = 5.

The larger absolute value is 12, which is positive → answer is 5.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Simplify −9 − 4.

Show answer

−9 − 4 = −(9 + 4) = −13 (both negative, add and keep the sign).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the value of −6 + (−3)?

  1. −9
  2. 3
  3. −3
  4. 9

2. What is the value of 8 − (−5)?

  1. −13
  2. 3
  3. 13
  4. −3

3. Which expression has a value of −24?

  1. 6 × (−4)
  2. −6 × (−4)
  3. 3 × (−8)
  4. −3 × 8
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. −6 + (−3) = −(6 + 3) = −9 → A.

2. 8 − (−5) = 8 + 5 = 13 → C.
Subtracting a negative is the same as adding a positive.

3. We want −24.
6 × (−4) = −24 → A.
−6 × (−4) = 24 (positive).
3 × (−8) = −24 → also works, but if you want a discussion, you can ask students to identify all correct choices.

You can turn problem 3 into a discussion: “Which choices give −24, and why?”
