Day 66 – Factoring (a ≠ 1)

Goal: Practice skills with Factoring (a ≠ 1).

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Factoring When a ≠ 1):
For ax² + bx + c, use the “ac method”: find two numbers that multiply to ac and add to b, split the middle term, then factor by grouping.

Example (General Algebra)

Factor 2x² + 7x + 3.

Step 1 – Compute ac: a = 2, c = 3 → ac = 6.

Step 2 – Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 7:

  • 1 · 6 = 6 and 1 + 6 = 7 → use 1 and 6.

Step 3 – Split the middle term using 1 and 6:

  • 2x² + 7x + 3 = 2x² + x + 6x + 3

Step 4 – Factor by grouping:

  • (2x² + x) + (6x + 3)
  • x(2x + 1) + 3(2x + 1)
  • (x + 3)(2x + 1)

So 2x² + 7x + 3 = (x + 3)(2x + 1).

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Factor: 3x² + 8x + 4

Show answer

ac = 3 · 4 = 12. Numbers that multiply to 12 and add to 8: 2 and 6.
3x² + 2x + 6x + 4 → (3x² + 2x) + (6x + 4)
x(3x + 2) + 2(3x + 2) → (x + 2)(3x + 2).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Factor: 2x² − 5x − 3

  1. (2x + 1)(x − 3)
  2. (2x − 1)(x + 3)
  3. (2x − 3)(x + 1)
  4. (2x + 3)(x − 1)

2. Factor: 4x² + 11x + 6

  1. (4x + 3)(x + 2)
  2. (4x + 2)(x + 3)
  3. (2x + 3)(2x + 2)
  4. (2x + 1)(2x + 6)

3. What is the first step in the ac method for factoring ax² + bx + c?

  1. Find the vertex.
  2. Divide everything by a.
  3. Find two numbers that multiply to ac and add to b.
  4. Multiply a and b together.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. ac = 2 · (−3) = −6; numbers that multiply to −6 and add to −5: −6 and 1.
2x² − 6x + x − 3 → 2x(x − 3) + 1(x − 3) → (2x + 1)(x − 3) → A.

2. ac = 4 · 6 = 24; numbers that multiply to 24 and add to 11: 3 and 8.
4x² + 3x + 8x + 6 → x(4x + 3) + 2(4x + 3) → (x + 2)(4x + 3) → A.

3. ac method starts by finding numbers that multiply to ac and add to b → C.

When a ≠ 1, the factoring puzzle is about ac (not just c). Once you split the middle term correctly, grouping does the rest.
