Day 58 – Vertex Form

Goal: Practice skills with Vertex Form.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Vertex Form):
Vertex form of a quadratic is y = a(x − h)² + k. The vertex of the parabola is (h, k).

Example (General Algebra)

Identify the vertex of y = 2(x − 3)² + 5.

Compare to y = a(x − h)² + k:

  • a = 2
  • h = 3
  • k = 5

Vertex: (h, k) = (3, 5).

The parabola opens upward because a = 2 > 0.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

What is the vertex of y = −(x + 4)² + 1?

Show answer

Rewrite x + 4 as (x − (−4)), so h = −4 and k = 1.
Vertex: (−4, 1).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the vertex of y = (x − 2)² − 9?

  1. (−2, −9)
  2. (2, −9)
  3. (−2, 9)
  4. (2, 9)

2. Which equation represents a parabola with vertex at (1, −3)?

  1. y = (x + 1)² − 3
  2. y = (x − 1)² − 3
  3. y = (x − 1)² + 3
  4. y = (x + 1)² + 3

3. For y = −4(x + 2)² + 7, which statement is true?

  1. The vertex is (−2, 7) and the parabola opens up.
  2. The vertex is (−2, 7) and the parabola opens down.
  3. The vertex is (2, −7) and the parabola opens up.
  4. The vertex is (2, −7) and the parabola opens down.
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. y = (x − h)² + k → h = 2, k = −9 → vertex (2, −9) → B.

2. Vertex (h, k) = (1, −3) → y = (x − 1)² − 3 → B.

3. h = −2, k = 7, a = −4 (opens down) → vertex (−2, 7), opens down → B.

Watch the sign: y = a(x − h)² + k means the vertex’s x-coordinate is the opposite of the sign you see inside the parentheses.
