Goal: Practice skills with Factoring (Basic).

Key Idea (Basic Factoring a = 1):
For quadratics like x² + bx + c, find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b. Use them to split the middle term and write two binomial factors.

Example (General Algebra)

Factor x² + 5x + 6.

Step 1 – Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5:

  • 2 · 3 = 6 and 2 + 3 = 5 → use 2 and 3.

Step 2 – Write the factors:

  • x² + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Factor: x² + 7x + 10

Show answer

Numbers that multiply to 10 and add to 7: 5 and 2.
x² + 7x + 10 = (x + 5)(x + 2).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which is a factorization of x² + 3x − 10?

  1. (x + 5)(x − 2)
  2. (x − 5)(x + 2)
  3. (x − 5)(x − 2)
  4. (x + 5)(x + 2)

2. Factor: x² − 9x + 18

  1. (x − 2)(x − 9)
  2. (x − 3)(x − 6)
  3. (x + 3)(x + 6)
  4. (x + 2)(x + 9)

3. Factor: x² − 4x − 12

  1. (x − 6)(x + 2)
  2. (x + 6)(x − 2)
  3. (x − 3)(x − 4)
  4. (x + 3)(x + 4)
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Look for numbers that multiply to −10 and add to 3 → 5 and −2: (x + 5)(x − 2) → A.

2. Multiply to 18 and add to −9 → −3 and −6: (x − 3)(x − 6) → B.

3. Multiply to −12 and add to −4 → −6 and 2: (x − 6)(x + 2) → A.

If a = 1, factoring is a “numbers puzzle”: look for two numbers that multiply to c and add to b.
