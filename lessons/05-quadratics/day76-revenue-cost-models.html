Key Idea (Revenue & Cost Models):
Revenue and cost can be modeled with functions of quantity x. Profit is P(x) = R(x) − C(x). A quadratic profit function often has a maximum profit at its vertex.

Example (General Algebra)

A company sells a product. The price per item depends on how many they sell: price = 20 − 0.5x, where x is the number of items. Revenue is price × quantity, so

R(x) = x(20 − 0.5x) = −0.5x² + 20x.

Suppose cost is C(x) = 5x + 40.

Question: What number of items maximizes profit?

Step 1 – Write the profit function:

  • P(x) = R(x) − C(x) = (−0.5x² + 20x) − (5x + 40)
  • P(x) = −0.5x² + 20x − 5x − 40 = −0.5x² + 15x − 40

Step 2 – Find the vertex of P(x):

  • a = −0.5, b = 15 → x = −b / (2a) = −15 / (2·−0.5) = −15 / −1 = 15

Profit is maximized when they sell 15 items.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

The revenue function for a concert is R(x) = −2x² + 40x, where x is the number of $10 discount coupons used. How many coupons maximize revenue?

Show answer

a = −2, b = 40 → x = −40 / (2·−2) = −40 / −4 = 10.
Revenue is maximized when 10 coupons are used.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A company’s revenue is given by R(x) = −x² + 12x. What is the x-value that gives maximum revenue?

  1. −6
  2. 0
  3. 6
  4. 12

2. For the function in question 1, what is the maximum revenue?

  1. $18
  2. $24
  3. $36
  4. $72

3. If profit is given by P(x) = −3x² + 30x − 25, what does the vertex represent in context?

  1. The quantity and maximum profit
  2. The starting profit
  3. The break-even point
  4. The minimum possible loss
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

For R(x) = −x² + 12x:

1. a = −1, b = 12 → x = −12 / (2·−1) = 6 → C.

2. R(6) = −(6)² + 12·6 = −36 + 72 = 36 → maximum revenue is $36 → C.

3. The vertex of a profit function gives the quantity that maximizes profit and the value of that maximum profit → A.

In real-world quadratic models, “maximum” usually means best outcome (highest revenue or profit) — that’s why the vertex is so important.
