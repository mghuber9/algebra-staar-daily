Day 63 – Axis of Symmetry

Goal: Practice skills with Axis of Symmetry.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Axis of Symmetry):
The axis of symmetry is a vertical line that cuts the parabola in half. For y = ax² + bx + c, the axis is x = −b / (2a).

Example (General Algebra)

Find the axis of symmetry of y = 2x² − 8x + 1.

Step 1 – Identify a and b:

  • a = 2, b = −8

Step 2 – Use x = −b / (2a):

  • x = −(−8) / (2 · 2) = 8 / 4 = 2

Axis of symmetry: x = 2.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Find the axis of symmetry for y = −x² + 4x − 3.

Show answer

a = −1, b = 4 → x = −b / (2a) = −4 / (−2) = 2.
Axis of symmetry: x = 2.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. What is the axis of symmetry of y = x² − 6x + 5?

  1. x = −3
  2. x = 3
  3. x = −6
  4. x = 6

2. The graph of a quadratic has x-intercepts at 2 and 10. What is the axis of symmetry?

  1. x = 4
  2. x = 6
  3. x = 8
  4. x = 12

3. The vertex of a parabola is at (−5, 7). What is the equation of its axis of symmetry?

  1. y = −5
  2. y = 7
  3. x = −5
  4. x = 7
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. x = −b / (2a) = −(−6) / 2 = 6 / 2 = 3 → B.

2. Axis is halfway between the intercepts: (2 + 10)/2 = 12/2 = 6 → B.

3. Axis passes through the vertex vertically → x = −5 → C.

The axis of symmetry always goes through the vertex and splits the parabola into mirror-image halves.
