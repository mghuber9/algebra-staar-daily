Day 68 – Difference of Squares

Goal: Practice skills with Difference of Squares.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Difference of Squares):
A pattern like a² − b² factors as (a − b)(a + b). Both terms must be perfect squares with a minus sign between them.

Example (General Algebra)

Factor x² − 25.

Step 1 – Recognize perfect squares:

  • x² is (x)²
  • 25 is (5)²

Step 2 – Use the pattern a² − b² = (a − b)(a + b):

  • x² − 25 = (x − 5)(x + 5)

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Factor: 9x² − 16

Show answer

9x² = (3x)² and 16 = 4².
9x² − 16 = (3x − 4)(3x + 4).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which expression is equivalent to 4x² − 81?

  1. (2x − 9)(2x + 9)
  2. (4x − 9)(x + 9)
  3. (2x − 81)(2x + 81)
  4. (x − 9)(4x + 9)

2. Factor: 25x² − 1

  1. (5x − 1)(5x − 1)
  2. (5x + 1)(5x + 1)
  3. (5x − 1)(5x + 1)
  4. (25x − 1)(x + 1)

3. Which expression cannot be factored as a difference of squares?

  1. x² − 36
  2. 16 − y²
  3. 9a² − 4b²
  4. x² + 36
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. 4x² = (2x)² and 81 = 9² → (2x − 9)(2x + 9) → A.

2. 25x² = (5x)² and 1 = 1² → (5x − 1)(5x + 1) → C.

3. Difference of squares needs a minus sign; x² + 36 is a sum, not a difference → D.

Look for “square minus square.” If it’s “square plus square,” it is not a real difference-of-squares pattern.
