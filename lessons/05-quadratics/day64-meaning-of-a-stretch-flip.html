Day 64 – Meaning of a (Stretch/Flip)

Goal: Practice skills with Meaning of a (Stretch/Flip).

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Meaning of a):
In y = a(x − h)² + k or y = ax² + bx + c, the coefficient a controls how “wide” or “narrow” the parabola is and whether it opens up or down.
  • a > 0 → opens up
  • a < 0 → opens down
  • |a| > 1 → narrower (vertical stretch)
  • 0 < |a| < 1 → wider (vertical shrink)

Example (General Algebra)

Compare the graphs of y = x² and y = 3x².

y = x²: Basic parabola opening up, “regular” width.

y = 3x²:

  • a = 3 (bigger than 1) → graph is narrower and still opens up.

So y = 3x² is a vertical stretch of y = x².

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Does y = −(1/2)x² open up or down? Is it wider or narrower than y = x²?

Show answer

a = −1/2 (negative) → opens down.
|a| = 1/2 < 1 → graph is wider than y = x².

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Which function represents a parabola that opens downward and is narrower than y = x²?

  1. y = −5x²
  2. y = −(1/3)x²
  3. y = 5x²
  4. y = (1/3)x²

2. The graph of y = 2(x − 1)² + 3 is compared to y = (x − 1)² + 3. What effect does the 2 have?

  1. Makes the parabola wider
  2. Makes the parabola narrower
  3. Shifts the graph left
  4. Shifts the graph up

3. Which graph description matches y = −4(x + 2)² − 1?

  1. Opens up, narrower than y = x²
  2. Opens up, wider than y = x²
  3. Opens down, narrower than y = x²
  4. Opens down, wider than y = x²
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. a must be negative and |a| > 1 → y = −5x² → A.

2. Multiplying by 2 (a = 2) is a vertical stretch → graph is narrower → B.

3. a = −4 (negative, |a| > 1) → opens down and is narrower → C.

Think of a as a “steepness” slider: big |a| means steep/narrow; small |a| means flat/wide. Negative a flips the whole graph upside down.
