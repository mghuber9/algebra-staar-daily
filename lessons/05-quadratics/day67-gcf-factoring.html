Day 67 – GCF Factoring

Goal: Practice skills with GCF Factoring.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (GCF Factoring):
Always check for a Greatest Common Factor (GCF) first. Factor it out to make the quadratic simpler before using other methods.

Example (General Algebra)

Factor 6x² − 12x.

Step 1 – Find the GCF of the terms:

  • Coefficients: GCF of 6 and 12 is 6.
  • Variables: both have x → at least x¹.
  • GCF is 6x.

Step 2 – Factor out 6x:

  • 6x² − 12x = 6x(x − 2)

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Factor: 9x² + 15x

Show answer

GCF of 9x² and 15x is 3x.
9x² + 15x = 3x(3x + 5).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Factor completely: 4x² + 8x

  1. 4x(x + 2)
  2. 4(x² + 2)
  3. 2x(2x + 4)
  4. x(4x + 8)

2. What is the GCF of 12x³ and 18x²?

  1. 6x²
  2. 6x³
  3. 3x²
  4. 2x

3. Factor completely: 5x³ − 10x²

  1. 5x²(x − 2)
  2. 5x(x² − 2)
  3. x²(5x − 10)
  4. 5(x³ − 2x²)
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. GCF is 4x → 4x(x + 2) → A.

2. GCF of coefficients: 12 and 18 → 6; lowest power of x → x² → 6x² → A.

3. GCF is 5x² → 5x²(x − 2) → A.

Factoring out the GCF first makes the numbers smaller and the quadratic easier to work with—never skip this step.
