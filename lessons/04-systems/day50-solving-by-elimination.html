Day 50 – Solving by Elimination

Goal: Practice skills with Solving by Elimination.

← Back to all lessons
Key Idea (Elimination):
In elimination, you add or subtract the equations to eliminate one variable. Often you multiply one or both equations first so that one variable cancels out.

Example (General Algebra)

Solve the system:

  • 2x + y = 9
  • 3x − y = 6

Step 1 – Add the equations to eliminate y:

  • (2x + y) + (3x − y) = 9 + 6
  • 5x = 15

Step 2 – Solve for x: x = 15 ÷ 5 = 3.

Step 3 – Substitute x back to find y:

  • Use 2x + y = 9 → 2(3) + y = 9 → 6 + y = 9 → y = 3

The solution is (3, 3).

Your Turn (General Algebra)

Solve by elimination:

  • x + y = 8
  • x − y = 2
Show answer

Add the equations: (x + y) + (x − y) = 8 + 2 → 2x = 10 → x = 5.
Plug back: x + y = 8 → 5 + y = 8 → y = 3.
Solution: (5, 3).

STAAR-Style Practice

1. Solve the system:

  • 2x + 3y = 13
  • 2x − y = 1
  1. (1, 4)
  2. (2, 3)
  3. (3, 2)
  4. (4, 1)

2. Which step would help eliminate y in the system below?

  • x + 2y = 7
  • 3x − 4y = 5
  1. Multiply the first equation by 2.
  2. Multiply the second equation by 2.
  3. Multiply the first equation by 3.
  4. Multiply the second equation by 3.

3. A system is given by 2x + y = 5 and 4x + 2y = 10. What can you conclude?

  1. The system has exactly one solution.
  2. The system has no solution.
  3. The system has infinitely many solutions.
  4. The solution is (0, 0).
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Subtract the second equation from the first, or line them up:
(2x + 3y) − (2x − y) = 13 − 1 → 4y = 12 → y = 3. Then 2x − y = 1 → 2x − 3 = 1 → 2x = 4 → x = 2 → (2, 3) → B.

2. To eliminate y, make coefficients opposites: 2y and −4y → multiply the first equation by 2 to get 2x + 4y = 14, then add to 3x − 4y = 5 → A.

3. The second equation is just 2 times the first, so both represent the same line → infinitely many solutions → C.

Elimination is usually fastest when you see matching or easily-matched coefficients on x or y—aim to cancel one variable with addition or subtraction.
