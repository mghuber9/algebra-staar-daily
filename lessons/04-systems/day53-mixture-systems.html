Key Idea (Mixture Systems):
Mixture problems combine two amounts with different “values” (like price or concentration). Use one equation for total amount and one for total value.

Example (General Algebra)

A candy store mixes $5-per-pound candy with $8-per-pound candy to make 20 pounds of a $7-per-pound mix. How many pounds of each type are used?

Step 1 – Define variables:

  • Let x = pounds of $5 candy.
  • Let y = pounds of $8 candy.

Step 2 – Total amount equation:

  • x + y = 20

Step 3 – Total value equation:

  • 5x + 8y = 7(20) = 140

Step 4 – Solve:

  • From x + y = 20 → x = 20 − y.
  • Substitute: 5(20 − y) + 8y = 140
  • 100 − 5y + 8y = 140 → 100 + 3y = 140 → 3y = 40 → y = 40/3 ≈ 13.33
  • Then x = 20 − 40/3 = 20/3 ≈ 6.67

Answer: About 6.67 lb of $5 candy and 13.33 lb of $8 candy.

Your Turn (General Algebra)

You want 10 liters of a 30% juice mixture using a 20% juice drink and a 50% juice drink. Let a = liters of 20% juice and b = liters of 50% juice. Write the system.

Show answer

Total amount: a + b = 10.
Total “juice” value: 0.20a + 0.50b = 0.30(10) = 3.

STAAR-Style Practice

1. A jeweler mixes 12-karat gold and 18-karat gold to make 10 grams of 16-karat gold. Let x = grams of 12-karat and y = grams of 18-karat. Which equation represents the total mass?

  1. 12x + 18y = 16
  2. x + y = 10
  3. 16x + 16y = 10
  4. x + y = 16

2. A coffee shop mixes $6-per-pound beans with $9-per-pound beans to create 15 pounds of a $8-per-pound blend. Which value equation is correct if x is pounds of $6 beans and y is pounds of $9 beans?

  1. 6x + 9y = 15
  2. 6x + 9y = 8
  3. 6x + 9y = 120
  4. 6x + 9y = 8(15)

3. In a mixture system, what do the coefficients like 5, 8, or 0.30 usually represent?

  1. Number of containers
  2. Prices, strengths, or percentages
  3. Time in minutes
  4. Just random numbers
Show STAAR-style answers and explanations

1. Total mass comes from adding the two parts → x + y = 10 → B.

2. Total value is price times amount for each plus: 6x + 9y = 8 · 15 → D.

3. The coefficients represent value per unit (price, concentration, etc.) → B.

Mixture systems are usually “amount + amount = total” and “value + value = total value.” Line those up and solve.
